• Bridge by Deloitte

    We had a lot of enterprise clients who wanted to move faster, so we built Bridge.


    It's a matchmaking platform for Fortune 500 companies and innovators (startups, universities, government agencies, etc.).

  • Koi

    My mom and her friends have trouble staying consistently active, and it's beginning to result in increased health costs.


    So I built a landing page and lean MVP to test a business idea to help middle-aged men and women motivate to stay active.


    The splash page uses CSS animations, and the prototype was built in Backbone.js/ React.js and includes a chat application built with Firebase.

  • HumanConnect

    I wanted to learn about data visualization and extracting movement data.


    HumanConnect allows users to visualize their movement, food intake, and location data - and share the information with select friends via a social network.


    It uses HumanAPI and its partner wellness applications to pull user wellness data. Currently we have it set up to work with the "Moves" app and "Misfit Wearables" apps and devices.


    This was a group project, so I got some good pair programming and Git experience.


  • Project Al

    80% of US health care costs stem from preventable diseases. This finally hit home when a coworker's father was battling congestive heart failure.


    The app allows doctors to remotely monitor their elderly patients with chronic illnesses and uses elements of machine learning, cognitive computing, and digital health device data aggregation.


    I built the business case, designed the prototype and roadmap, pitched for funding, and vetted technology partners.
